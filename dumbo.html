Dumbo Octopus

On the other side of the scary deep-sea creature spectrum, we have the Grimpoteuthis, nicknamed the Dumbo Octopus, due to its ear-like fins on either side of its head, resembling the flying Disney-elephant, Dumbo. This soft, round little guy dwells more than three kilometres under the surface, where it gently drifts along, eating whatever worms, shellfish, copepods and so on, it happens to pass by. These smaller animals are what it must prey upon, since it itself is very small. The average dumbo octopus weighs up to one kilo weight and 30cm length.

This small creature has an expected lifespan of around four years and can be found in The Philippines, west coast America, New Zealand, the Gulf of Mexico, among others. Predators include sharks, dolphins, and marine animals, primarily. The population is healthy, and the species is in no way considered endangered.
