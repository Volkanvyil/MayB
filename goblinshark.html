goblin shark

The goblin shark is a giant predator lurking 270 to 960 meters from the ocean’s surface in most of Asia and South Africa. This apex predator weighs up to 210 kilos and can grow up to 3,7 meters long. This animal’s most remarkable feature is its mouth. It has the ability to detach its jaw and launch its razor-sharp teeth at any unsuspecting prey. Being a carnivore, it only eats other animals, such as fish, stingray, and other sharks.

The goblin shark’s skeleton is very small compared to the rest of its body, giving it a lot of wiggly excess skin. The goblin shark population is healthy and nowhere near endangered status.
