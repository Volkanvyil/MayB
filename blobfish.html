blobfish

The notorious psychrolutidae, commonly referred to as the blobfish, has become quite popular on the internet over the past few years for its unofficial title as the ugliest animal.

This animal is a grey fish, with rough protective skin, used for protection. A grown adult weighs around 9 kilos and is usually around 30cm long. They reside in the Indian, Pacific, and Atlantic oceans, between 1 to 3 kilometres from the surface. The blobfish is a predator, hunting by using its grey colour to blend in with the sea floor. There it lies still, with its mouth open, waiting for crustaceans or other small animals to randomly wonder inside.

When the blobfish leaves water its skin will start to soften up, become lose and turn a fleshy pink. Due to this fish inhabiting the same area as a lot of tuna and other commonly eaten fish, the blobfish population is rapidly declining because of overfishing.
